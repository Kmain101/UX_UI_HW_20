The Challenge

SAMHSA, Substance Abuse And Mental Health Services Administration website is overall good but based on research, I discovered that the colors and visuals aren't uplifting and not inspirational. The primary and secondary menu is overwhelming with how many links the menu has and can be hard to navigate through. When you think of a website that is for mental health and substance abuse you want it to be inspiring and positive as well as, simple and easy to navigate.

Kitten Rescue San Antonio NW aims to rescue and nourish kittens in need and provide them with their forever home. We have observed that the current website is not meeting potential adopters needs & expectations when trying to scan through the current design to find necessary information, which is causing them to feel apprehensive about moving forward with the adoption process.

How might we instill confidence in potential adopters to allow for successful adoptions and community support of Kitten Rescue San Antonio NW?

Research & Analysis

Research starts with analyzing data and getting user insights. For each project I created a user persona to identify the users as well as find their pain points.

Concepts, Wireframes

Beginning with low fidelity wireframes and focusing on navigation is key when doing a redesign. Making sure the menu system makes sense and the users are able to understand it with ease.

Visual Design & Prototype

After getting insights from users and more research I begin to focus on the visual design and prototyping. Starting with a style guide to plan out my UI design with, buttons/variants, font style/size, images.

High Fidelity Design

After the ideation process I add more visual designs and content from my style guides turning my wireframes into hi fidelity.

Test: Validation, Usability, Feedback

I then started more usability testing on my high fidelity prototype to help me understand how users navigate and help me see from a different perspective by hearing their general thoughts, also gives me more inspiration on how I can make it perform and look better.

My group and I conducted a usability test on our mid fidelity prototype. We asked the users to complete the same task we did on the previous test on the original Kitten Rescue website asking the users to find the adoption fee in 45 seconds. When the users did the test on our prototype on average the users were able to find the adoption fee in 16.5 seconds. That is a huge improvement compared to the original test with an average on 1 minute 45 seconds.

Conclusion

Providing trustworthy branding and simplistic design, this helps users feel confident and comfortable so they can find required information and able to navigate the system with ease.
